Another project I have recently rebooted is one which I am very excited about. As a software designer, it's key that you get up to speed with others and make sure that you're on the same page when discussing a concept. Frequently this can be weakest link in a project, and often times there are a series of miscommunications that may occur along the way that can set you and others back quite a ways. This is not necessarily our fault, as we are only human. The programming and scripting languages we work with are a concession we must give in order to be able to work with the various devices around us. A computer cannot fully understand us out of the box, and likewise we cannot understand it without learning the means of communicating with it. These languages can be understood in time, and to some they become as rudimentary as any conversation in a spoken language, but there is a faster means to understand what we are working with. However replacing a language altogether is a rather large task and that's not the result that I am looking.

What I am looking for is a way to change the way we interpret that language. To take the structure of a file, a series of files, or an entire project, and represent it in a means which is universally understood. On another note the latest application I released to Google Play is called "Hangman Azul". The purpose is in the name, it's a hangman game! However the azul portion bears significance as well. Azul is spanish for blue, and that color would be the basis for the theme of this app. The background is blue and the usage is through bluetooth. The bluetooth part is the primary function, as this is only a multiplayer game. I attempted to start something like this over a year ago, but was unable to realize it. Earlier this year I revisited the idea and was able to finally get it set up. It's currently at an early release, at Version 0.2.1, but it has been in the works for some time now.
